Demystifying Buzzwords

Tech + Data =
Buzzword Extravaganza

  • Tech and data are full of buzzwords
  • Buzzwords come and go, and sometime make comebacks
  • This talk covers four buzzwords/phrases

#1: Big Data

Big Data: An Enterprise Term

  • Big Data has become less trendy in recent years
  • General: Silicon Valley → population → corporate world
  • Big Data: outdated to us, still a thing in bigger/slower companies

Big Data Knows All

  • This comic captures the spirit of big data
  • Read out comic
  • Not an exaggeration – CBA as an example

Big Data... or just a Small Box?

  • Is Big Data really a thing?
  • A gigabyte thirty years ago versus today
  • Many valuable datasets are small enough to fit on a beefy server
  • Better to get more computing power before investing in exotic techniques

#2: Data Science

Data Science: 2012 Edition

  • Close to my heart – data scientist since 2012
  • Meaning shifted over time
  • Intersection of software engineering and statistics was six years ago

Data Science: Too Exclusive?

  • Intersection-based definitions led to many Venn diagrams like this one
  • Data scientist as a unicorn
  • Many people joined the field, leading to a more inclusive definition

Data Science: 2018 Edition

...we can define data science as a field that deals with description, prediction, and causal inference from data in a manner that is both domain-independent and domain-aware, with the ultimate goal of supporting decisions.

#3: Artificial Intelligence

  • One of the hottest terms!

AI: More Venn Diagram Fun?

  • Data loves Venn
  • Not attractive, but captures important intersections
  • AI doesn't mean much, as intelligence isn't well-defined
  • Calculators are intelligent
  • Google search is AI
  • AI: Anything that computers can't do yet?

AI: The Hype Won't Die

  • Long history with ups and downs through the years
  • 1950s: decade or two to AGI? Followed by disappointment
  • We're definitely going up these days

AI and I (2004-2018)

  • Bachelor's from 2004 include AI course and project
  • PhD application in 2008: Something in AI?
  • Graduation 2012: AI experts are silly? Data scientists are sexier
  • Today: AI expert is better?
  • Doesn't matter in terms of substance, but branding is important

#4: Machine Learning

Machine Learning by Example

  • Broadly: Pattern extraction
  • Data: Images tagged as car/not-car
  • Traditional: Data scientist to generate features
  • ML algorithm: Extract patterns
  • New image: car/not-car
  • DL: Reduce need for manual work to generate features for some problems

ML: A Solved Problem?

  • Solved problem given cloud-based services and libraries?
  • Yes, that's why don't talk about algorithms much
  • Defining problems and collecting the right data are more important and hard than ML
